in his first go as a hollywood director, henry brommell whips an enthralling yarn that is all of penetrating relatable marital issues with melancholic authenticity, and lacing such with an equally absorbing subplot of a father-son hit-man business. the film is directed astutely and consists of a wonderfully put together cast as well as a swift, family-conscious screenplay (also by brommell) that brings life to an otherwise fatigued genre. as a bonus, 'panic' delivers subtle, acerbic humor
