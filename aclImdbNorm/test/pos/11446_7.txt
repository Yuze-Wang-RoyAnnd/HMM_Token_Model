much more than any other film from that period, night of the demons represents the brainless and hugely enjoyable horror pastiche. it's undemanding fun with loads of nasty make-up effects, gorgeous looking (and horny) teenagers and adorable clich'e-elements. a group of party animals, led by the alternative angela, goes to celebrate halloween in an abandoned funeral home that carries an eerie urban legend. it all starts out typical and "innocent" with dancing, drinking and the occasional flirt between youngsters that can't keep their hormones under control, but pretty soon a bloodthirsty demon possesses the hostess. in the most ingenious ways you've ever seen, the rest of the cast gets slaughtered viciously only to return as hideous creatures prowling for blood. the thing that makes this film better than most cheesy 80's horror films is finds a good balance between light-headed camp and atmospheric horror. some sequences really are creepy and the funeral house setting supplies night of the demons with an excellent tone. director tenney makes great use of the set pieces (coffins, a crematorium, endless dark hallways
